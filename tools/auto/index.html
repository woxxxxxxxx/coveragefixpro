Frequently Asked Questions

What factors affect my auto insurance premium? ▼
Key factors include your age, driving history, vehicle make/model/year, annual mileage, ZIP code, credit score (in most states), and the coverage levels and deductibles you choose. Use our Car Insurance Premium Estimator to see how these variables affect your rate.
How can I lower my auto insurance rates? ▼
Common discounts include bundling home and auto, maintaining a clean driving record (good driver discount), insuring multiple vehicles, taking a defensive driving course, raising your deductible, and qualifying for low-mileage programs. Use our calculators to quantify each opportunity.
Do I need GAP insurance? ▼
GAP insurance is worth considering if you financed more than 80% of your car's value, your loan term is 60+ months, or you drive significant miles. Use our GAP Insurance Calculator to see if your loan balance exceeds your car's current market value.
How long does a DUI affect insurance rates? ▼
A DUI typically affects your insurance rates for 3–7 years depending on your state. The surcharge decreases over time as the incident moves further into the past. Our DUI Rate Calculator shows the impact at different time intervals.
Is full coverage required? ▼
Lenders typically require comprehensive and collision coverage if you have an auto loan or lease. Once you own the car outright, you can drop to liability-only. Use our Full Coverage vs Liability Calculator to decide when it makes financial sense to switch.
