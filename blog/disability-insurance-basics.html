Disability Insurance Basics: Protecting Income When Work Stops

Compare waiting periods, benefit periods, own-occupation terms, and taxable benefits.

Compare waiting periods, benefit periods, own-occupation terms, and taxable benefits. Use this guide to prepare better insurance questions before speaking with a licensed professional or comparing policy documents.

What to Confirm First

Start with the policy type, limits, deductible, exclusions, waiting periods, endorsements, household or business changes, and the evidence you would need during a claim.

Build a Base Case and a Claim Case

Read the policy once for normal cost and once for a realistic claim scenario. Coverage gaps often appear when a loss is excluded, a limit is too low, or documentation is missing.

  • Keep declarations pages and endorsements together.
  • Compare limits and exclusions, not only premiums.
  • Review coverage after major life or business changes.

Common Mistakes

Avoid assuming a cheaper premium is better, ignoring named exclusions, forgetting riders, or waiting until after a claim to build an inventory or document timeline.
